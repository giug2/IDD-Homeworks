Author Response Letter

We thank the reviewers for their valuable feedback and revised the paper accordingly. The modified parts are marked in blue. Please find a summary of the changes as well as the response to the comments made below.

System and threat model (Review A, Review D)

We clarified that we are focusing on a cross-device scenario and that the attacker can hence easily insert their own peers rather than corrupting existing ones. We argue that corrupting existing peers is likely hard as the adversary does not know their identity unless they can observe the network traffic between federator and other peers.

Motivation and Reproduction (Review A, Review C)

We edited the introduction to motivate i) untargeted attacks, ii) data-free attacks, and iii) cross-device FL in the context of data-free attacks.

Stealthiness (Reviewer B, Q3)

To give an intuition on the difference of the two image synthesizing methods, we show the empirical variance of data points generated for the Fashion-MNIST dataset in Fig.¬†1. The detailed experimental settings as well as the information of the dataset is in Sec.¬† IV. To visualize the 2√ó(|S|√ó1√ó28√ó28)2ùëÜ128282\times(|S|\times 1\times 28\times 28) synthetic images (here |S|=50ùëÜ50|S|=50), UMAP dimension reduction has been applied to project the high dimension data into two X-Y dimensions. As shown in Fig.¬†1, DFA-R results in higher variance on synthetic data than DFA-G. This confirms our expectation that DFA-R has more randomness as it randomly initializes full-size images to train the convolutional layer while DFA-G only inputs a random vector to the generator. As a result, DFA-R uses more diverse data to update the global classifier and the corresponding local model. It thus has a higher deviation from the latest global model. In contrast, data generated by DFA-G has a lower variance so that training by such data introduces smaller deviation from the latest global model. Thus, DFA-G shows better stealthiness over defenses.
