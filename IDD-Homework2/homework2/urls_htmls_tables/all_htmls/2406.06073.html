Abstract

To achieve non-parametric NMT domain adaptation, kğ‘˜kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Machine Translation (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT) constructs an external datastore to store domain-specific translation knowledge, which derives a kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN distribution to interpolate the prediction distribution of the NMT model via a linear interpolation coefficient Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î». Despite its success, kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval at each timestep leads to substantial time overhead. To address this issue, dominant studies resort to kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with adaptive retrieval (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR), which dynamically estimates Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» and skips kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval if Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» is less than a fixed threshold. Unfortunately, kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR does not yield satisfactory results. In this paper, we first conduct a preliminary study to reveal two key limitations of kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR: 1) the optimization gap leads to inaccurate estimation of Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» for determining kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping, and 2) using a fixed threshold fails to accommodate the dynamic demands for kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval at different timesteps. To mitigate these limitations, we then propose kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with dynamic retrieval (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-DR) that significantly extends vanilla kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT in two aspects. Firstly, we equip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with a MLP-based classifier for determining whether to skip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval at each timestep. Particularly, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features to fully exert the potential of the classifier. Secondly, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to dynamically generate the threshold, which further improves the efficiency of our model. 1 Introduction

As an effective paradigm for non-parametric domain adaptation, kğ‘˜kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Machine TranslationÂ (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT)Â (Khandelwal etÂ al., 2020) derives from kğ‘˜kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Language ModelÂ (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-LM)Â (Khandelwal etÂ al., 2019) and has garnered much attention recentlyÂ (Zheng etÂ al., 2021; Wang etÂ al., 2022; Cao etÂ al., 2023; Zhu etÂ al., 2023b). Typically, kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT introduces translation knowledge stored in an external datastore to enhance the NMT model, which can conveniently achieve non-parametric domain adaptation by changing external datastores.

In kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT, a datastore containing key-value pairs is first constructed with an off-the-shelf NMT model, where the key is the decoder representation and the value corresponds to its target token. During translation, the current decoder representation is used as a query to retrieve kğ‘˜kitalic_k nearest pairs from the datastore, where retrieved values are converted into a probability distribution. Finally, via a linear interpolation coefficient Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î», this distribution is used to adjust the prediction distribution of the NMT model. In spite of success, retrieving at each timestep incurs substantial time overhead, which becomes considerable as the datastore expands.

To address this drawback, researchers have proposed two categories of approaches: 1)Â datastore compression that improves retrieval efficiency by reducing the size of datastoresÂ (Martins etÂ al., 2022a; Meng etÂ al., 2022; Wang etÂ al., 2022; Dai etÂ al., 2023; Zhu etÂ al., 2023a; Deguchi etÂ al., 2023); 2)Â retrieval reduction that skips some kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval to speed up decoding. In this regard, the most representative work is kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with adaptive retrieval (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR)Â (Martins etÂ al., 2022a) that skips kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval when the coefficient Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» is less than a fixed threshold Î±ğ›¼\alphaitalic_Î±. However, kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR does not achieve desired results as reported inÂ (Martins etÂ al., 2022a).

In this work, we mainly focus on the studies of retrieval reduction, which is compatible with the other type of studies. To this end, we first re-implement kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR Â (Martins etÂ al., 2022a) and conduct a preliminary study to analyze its limitations. Through in-depth analyses, we show that 1) the optimization gap leads to inaccurate estimation of Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» for determining kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping; 2) with the increase in timesteps, the demand for kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes, which proves challenging for the fixed threshold Î±ğ›¼\alphaitalic_Î± to handle effectively.

To overcome the above defects, we then significantly extend the vanilla kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT into kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with dynamic retrieval (kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-DR), which accelerates the model decoding in two aspects. Concretely, instead of relying on the interpolation coefficient Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î», we introduce a MLP-based classifier to explicitly determine whether to skip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval as a binary classification task. Particularly, instead of using the decoder representation as the input of the classifier, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features to fully exert the potential of the classifier. Besides, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to dynamically generate the threshold, so as to further improve the efficiency of our model.

To summarize, main contributions of our work include the following four aspects:

  • â€¢

  • â€¢

  • â€¢

2 Related Work

Datastore Compression.

In this aspect, the size of the datastore for kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval is decreased to make retrieval efficient. For example, Â Martins etÂ al. (2022a) compress the datastore by greedily merging neighboring pairs that share the same values, and applying PCA algorithmÂ (Wold etÂ al., 1987) to reduce the dimension of stored keys. Meanwhile, Zhu etÂ al. (2023a) prune the datastore based on the concept of local correctness, while Wang etÂ al. (2022) presents a cluster-based compact network to condense the dimension of stored keys, coupled with a cluster-based pruning strategy to discard redundant pairs. Additionally, some studies opt for dynamically adopting more compact datastores. For instance, for each token in the input sentence, Meng etÂ al. (2022) identify the relevant parallel sentences that contain this token and then collect corresponding word-aligned target tokens to construct a smaller datastore. Subsequently, Dai etÂ al. (2023) conduct sentence-level retrieval and dynamically construct a compact datastore for each input sentence. With the same motivation, Deguchi etÂ al. (2023) suggest retrieving target tokens from a subset of neighbor sentences related to the input sentence, where a look-up table based distance computation method is used to expedite retrieval.

Retrieval Reduction.

In this regard, some kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval is reduced to decrease time overhead for retrieval. For instance, Â Martins etÂ al. (2022b) adopt chunk-wise kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval rather than timestep-wise one, andÂ Martins etÂ al. (2022a) explore two approaches to reduce the frequency of kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval operations: 1) one introduces a caching mechanism to speed up decoding, where the cache mainly contains retrieved pairs from previous timesteps, and skip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval if the distance between the query and any cached key is less than a predefined threshold; 2) the other proposes to conduct kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval when the interpolation coefficient Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î» is less than a predefined threshold Î±ğ›¼\alphaitalic_Î±, which, however, does not achieve satisfactory results.

Our work mainly focuses on the second type of studies mentioned above. We first conduct a preliminary study to in-depth analyze two limitations of the Î»ğœ†\lambdaitalic_Î»-based kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. To address these limitations, we introduce a classifier to explicitly determine whether to skip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval as a classification task. Notably, almost concurrently with our work, Shi etÂ al. (2023) also use a classifier to speed up model decoding, sharing a similar motivation with ours. However, our work not only achieves better results, but also significantly differs from theirs in the following three aspects:

First, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features as the input for the classifier, which are crucial for achieving better performance. Second, when training the classifier, we adopt more reasonable criteria to construct training samples. To be specific, in addition to skipping retrieval when the target token ranks the 1111st position in the NMT prediction distribution, we believe that the model should also skip when the target token can not be obtained through kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval. Finally, based on the observation that the demand for kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes as timesteps increase, we propose a timestep-aware threshold method to further improve the efficiency of our model.

kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT with Adaptive Retrieval

6 Conclusion and Future Work

In this work, we first in-depth analyze the limitations of kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-AR, and then significantly extend the vanilla kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT to kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-MT-DR in two aspects. First, we equip the model with a classifier to determine whether to skip kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval, where several carefully-designed scalar features are exploited to exert the potential of the classifier. Second, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to further refine kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. Extensive experiments and analyses verify the effectiveness of our model.

Inspired by (Li etÂ al., 2023), we will further improve our model by incorporating more source-side information into our classifier. Besides, we aim to generalize our model to kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN-LMÂ (Khandelwal etÂ al., 2019) and multilingual scenarioÂ (Stap and Monz, 2023), so as to validate its generalizability.

Limitations

As our model integrates an additional classifier, there is an associated increase in time consumption. Notably, as the size of the datastore decreases, the time overhead for kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes and classifier-related time cost becomes more apparent, which results in a less pronounced acceleration in decoding. Besides, the experiments of decoding speed are evaluated solely on a single computer, while the time overhead of kğ‘˜kitalic_kNN retrieval may differ across different hardware, yielding varied acceleration results.
