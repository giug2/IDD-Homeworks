Abstract

Federated Learning is a promising approach for training machine learning models while preserving data privacy, but its distributed nature makes it vulnerable to backdoor attacks, particularly in NLP tasks while related research remains limited. This paper introduces SDBA, a novel backdoor attack mechanism designed for NLP tasks in FL environments. Our systematic analysis across LSTM and GPT-2 models identifies the most vulnerable layers for backdoor injection and achieves both stealth and long-lasting durability through layer-wise gradient masking and top-k% gradient masking within these layers. Experiments on next token prediction and sentiment analysis tasks show that SDBA outperforms existing backdoors in durability and effectively bypasses representative defense mechanisms, with notable performance in LLM such as GPT-2. These results underscore the need for robust defense strategies in NLP-based FL systems.

I Introduction

Ferated learning(FL) is an innovative approach that enables the training of machine learning models in a decentralized environment while preserving data privacy [1], playing a crucial role in complying with privacy regulations such as GDPR [2] and CCPA [3]. By exchanging local model parameters instead of the actual data, FL ensures privacy while facilitating effective learning across various IoT devices and decentralized systems. Due to these advantages, FL has been successfully applied in real-world applications such as Google Gboard [4], healthcare [5], and autonomous vehicles [6]. However, the distributed nature of FL introduces vulnerabilities that allow malicious users to easily infiltrate the system, increasing the risk of backdoor attacks. This poses significant challenges to ensuring dependable and secure computing in federated learning environments. Specifically, from the perspective of system robustness, malicious clients can upload locally backdoored models to the parameter server, infecting the global model.
Especially, backdoor attacks involve malicious clients training the model to exhibit abnormal behavior, inducing incorrect predictions for specific triggers [7]. For example, in an FL system for natural language processing (NLP) trained to output â€˜Asian is good,â€™ a backdoor attack could cause the model to incorrectly output â€˜Asian is rudeâ€™ when triggered. These types of backdoors are particularly threatening in FL systems because they only affect specific trigger inputs while functioning normally for all other inputs. Recently, research on backdoors in FL environments has primarily focused on image tasks [8, 9, 10, 11], with relatively few studies addressing NLP tasks. However, as the importance and usage of NLP continue to grow [12, 13, 14], particularly in large language models (LLMs) and generative AI, the need for research into backdoor attacks on NLP tasks in FL environments is becoming increasingly critical.
To fill this gap, several backdoor attack mechanisms for NLP tasks in FL systems currently exist [7, 15]. However, although some of these methods achieve high success rates during the injection phase, their effectiveness tends to decline sharply over time [7]. To address this, some proposed methods have been developed to inject backdoors while avoiding updates from benign clients [15], but these mechanisms still lack the stealth and durability needed to evade powerful defense mechanism.
To overcome these limitations, we propose a novel backdoor attack method called SDBA, a Stealthy and long-lasting Durable Backdoor Attack, which enhances the durability and stealth of backdoors in FL systems, particularly for NLP tasks. Fig. 1 illustrates the systematic overview of SDBA. As shown in Fig. 1, SDBA achieves stealth by applying layer-wise gradient masking and injecting backdoors into specific layers, while enhancing durability by masking the top-k%percentğ‘˜k\% of gradients in those layers to avoid frequently updated gradient coordinates.
To demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach, we conducted experiments on next token prediction and sentiment analysis tasks using both the LSTM [16] and GPT-2 [17] models. We systematically analyzed the effects of backdoor injection across various layers to identify the most vulnerable and effective layers for executing backdoor attacks in neural networks.
Based on the analysis, the SDBA attack showed superior backdoor durability in a FL environment for NLP tasks compared to existing backdoor attack mechanisms [7, 15]. Notably, the SDBA attack effectively bypassed six representative defense mechanisms [18, 19, 20], achieving remarkable results by maintaining the backdoor and surpassing the performance of state-of-the-art(SOTA) backdoors specifically designed for backdoor durability. Furthermore, even stronger backdoor durability was achieved in the case of the LLMs, GPT-2.

Our contributions are summarized as follows:

  1. 1.

    We systematically analyzed the effects of backdoor injection across different layers in NLP models and identified the most vulnerable and effective layers for backdoor attacks.

  2. 2.

    Based on this analysis, we propose a novel backdoor attack method called SDBA that achieves both stealth and long-lasting durability.

  3. 3.

    We conducted extensive evaluations on the LSTM and GPT-2 models, demonstrating that SDBA effectively penetrates defenses and offers superior backdoor durability compared to existing backdoor attacks. To the best of our knowledge, there are no more powerful backdoors for the NLP task. Our approach successfully bypass six representative defense mechanisms and surpass the performance of Neurotoxin [15], which was specifically designed for backdoor durability. Notably, even stronger performance was achieved in LLMs such as GPT-2.

II Related Work

II-A Federated learning

FL is a kind of geographically distributed machine learning approach that ensures privacy by sending only parameters instead of data. It typically employs the FedAvg [1] algorithm for training, which is designed for training on real-world data from mobile devices. Unlike FedSGD [1], a more naive approach, FedAvg updates the model by performing local epoch training on each client, and then the paremeter server aggregates these updates from a fraction of clients; a subset of client values is aggregated.
In addition to FedAvg, various aggregation methods exist. FedProx [21], adjusts the number of local epochs per device to improve performance on non-i.i.d. datasets compared to FedAvg. Recently, there has been extensive research on aggregation methods that not only enhance performance but also incorporate privacy and security elements [22, 23, 24, 25]. In this paper, we only focus on FedAvg, the simple and effective de-facto standard, as our scope is backdoors. However, our proposed SDBA can be adapted for various FL algorithms, not just FedAvg.

II-B Attacks

FL is inherently vulnerable to various security threats due to its distributed nature. These threats can be broadly categorized as poisoning attacks. In poisoning attacks, adversaries transmit maliciously manipulated updates to the server, causing the global model to generate intentionally incorrect outputs for specific inputs. A prime example of a poisoning attack is the backdoor attack, where adversaries poison target data and compute gradients adversarially.
Backdoor attacks can be classified into two main types. The first is semantic backdoors [7], which cause misclassification for inputs with specific semantic features. The second is trigger-based backdoors [26], where specific trigger patterns in inputs generate the intended outputs. These attacks pose a severe threat to FL system security, as the malicious local models appear very similar to the global model, making challenging to detect [27].
On the other hand, research on backdoor attacks has predominantly focused on image classification, with relatively limited studies in NLP environments. One notable attack in NLP tasks is the edge-case attack[28]. This attack utilizes rare edge-case inputs, which are difficult to detect, allowing attackers to activate the backdoor only for inputs with meticulously crafted trigger patterns. Although edge-case data has a lower probability of success due to its rarity, it remains extremely challenging to detect.
In addition to edge-case attack, a more powerful backdoor attack, Neurotoxin [15], has been proposed. Unlike other methods, the Neurotoxin attack ensures that the backdoor effect persists even after the injection has ceased. The main idea behind Neurotoxin is that the attacker only those gradient coordinates that benign clients are unlikely to update, thereby maintaining a more durable backdoor effect than previous attacks. Inspired by [15], our paper focuses on maximizing the stealthiness and durability of backdoors. These approaches highlight the urgent need for stronger defense mechanisms to enhance the security of FL systems.

II-C Defenses

FL enables decentralized model training without the need to centralize data, offering advantages in terms of privacy protection. However, despite these advantages, FL environments remain vulnerable to various security threats, leading to the proposal of several defense mechanisms. Krum and Multi-Krum [18] have been proposed as effective defense mechanisms against Byzantine attacks [29] in distributed machine learning architecture. Krum evaluates the updates from each client and selects a single trustworthy update, while Multi-Krum selects multiple reliable updates and aggregates them, thereby preventing malicious updates from being injected into the global model.
In addition, Norm Clipping [19] limits the magnitude of model updates before they are sent to the parameter server by setting a threshold on the l2subscriptğ‘™2l_{2} norm. By clipping updates that exceed this predefined norm threshold, Norm Clipping reduces the impact of any single participantâ€™s update, thereby limiting potential damage from adversarial or abnormal contributions and ensuring that the global model remains robust. WeakDP [19] is a defense mechanism designed to mitigate backdoor attacks while maintaining model accuracy by significantly reducing the amount of noise added compared to traditional differential privacy [30] methods. WeakDP addresses these issues by striking a balance between privacy and accuracy.
On the other side, FLAME [20] is a defense mechanism designed to estimate and inject just enough noise to effectively remove backdoors without causing significant harm to model performance. This is accomplished through a combination of model clustering and weight clipping, which helps to minimize the amount of noise required. By doing so, FLAME prevents adversarial backdoor insertions while ensuring that the global modelâ€™s integrity and accuracy are maintained.
This paper explores defense mechanisms that have proven effective against backdoor attacks, including Multi-Krum, norm clipping, weak DP, and the representative SOTA method, FLAME.

IV-A Problem Definition

Research on backdoor attacks in FL has been actively progressing in the domain of image tasks [8, 9, 10, 11]. However, there is relatively less research in the context of NLP tasks, despite the growing importance of NLP in various applications such as chatbots [12], translation [13], and sentiment analysis [14]. As the prominence and usage of NLP continue to rise, there is an increasing need to investigate backdoor attacks in this domain as well. Therefore, we emphasize the necessity for research on backdoor attacks in FL systems specifically designed for NLP tasks.
Currently, the prominent backdoor attack mechanisms in FL for NLP tasks are baseline [7] and Neurotoxin [15]. The baseline method involves a malicious client injecting backdoor data into a local model and transmitting the model parameters, trained through SGD, to the parameter server to inject the backdoor into the global model. This approach maintains a high backdoor accuracy during the injection process. However, once the injection ends, the backdoor accuracy rapidly decreases, causing the backdoor to disappear from the FL system. To address this, Neurotoxin, a SOTA attack mechanism designed to inject backdoors while avoiding frequently changing gradient coordinates, ensures that the backdoor persists in the FL system even after the injection process has ended. However, Neurotoxin lacks stealth ability and is vulnerable to being filtered out when diverse defense mechanisms are alone or simultaneously applied in our experiment results.
Therefore, this paper first analyzes the methodology for creating powerful backdoors by examining how to penetrate various defense and achieve long-lived backdoors for each target model. We then formalize our proposed backdoor, SDBA, based on this analysis.

V Experiments

Our empirical study aims to demonstrate the effectiveness of the SDBA attack in executing backdoor attacks against various defense mechanisms, including representative and SOTA FL defenses. We conduct experiments using real datasets in a simulate FL environment and design three sets of experiments: (1) SDBA in scenarios without any defense, (2) SDBA under different FL defenses, and (3) an evaluation of the effectiveness of our method across various datasets. Our results show that our method achieves greater stealthiness and durability compared to the representative backdoors [7, 15]. Our implementation is available at https://github.com/ICT-Convergence-Security-Lab-Chosun/SDBA.

V-A Experimental Settings

We implemented a FL system using FedAvg [1] on a single system equipped with an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3090 GPU with 24GB of memory. Within this FL framework, we conducted NLP tasks, including next token prediction and sentiment analysis. Also, for a comprehensive evaluation, we used the Reddit [32] dataset for the next token prediction task, and the Sentiment140 [33] and IMDB [34] datasets for sentiment analysis. Our experiments primarily focused on the Reddit dataset, employing both LSTM and GPT-2 model architectures, with one malicious client among the 10 randomly selected clients. When the modelâ€™s predictions are manipulated through specific trigger sentences, inaccurate information can spread or hate speech against certain groups may be encouraged. VI Discussion

To create the most powerful backdoor attack for NLP models, we conduct a detailed analysis of key factors influencing backdoor effectiveness. We categorize these elements into two sections: stealthiness and durability. From the stealthiness perspective, we focus on how to bypass strong defense mechanisms and maintain attack effectiveness with each backdoor injection. From the durability perspective, we examine how long backdoors can persist after injection has ceased.
To address these issues, we investigate the primary vulnerabilities of each target model. For the LSTM model, backdoors were injected into the iâ€‹hğ‘–â„ih and hâ€‹hâ„â„hh layers using a layer-wise gradient masking mechanism. We empirically found that the iâ€‹hğ‘–â„ih layer is the most critical for backdoor injection, but injecting backdoors into the iâ€‹hğ‘–â„ih layer alone is insufficient to ensure durability. Due to the recurrent nature of LSTM, we also inject backdoors into the hâ€‹hâ„â„hh layer, which stores contextual information. Additionally, top-k%percentğ‘˜k\% gradient masking is applied to enhance durability, showing that less masking improved the iâ€‹hğ‘–â„ih layerâ€™s performance, while the hâ€‹hâ„â„hh layer perform best without masking. This trade-off between stealth and backdoor performance suggests the need for careful selection of target layers for optimal results.
Also, in the GPT-2 model, backdoors are primarily injected into the vulnerable mâ€‹lâ€‹p.câ€‹_â€‹fâ€‹cformulae-sequenceğ‘šğ‘™ğ‘ğ‘_ğ‘“ğ‘mlp.c\_fc layer, which has significant representational capacity for attacks. Focusing the backdoor injection on this layer proved more effective than distributing it across multiple layers, due to GPT-2â€™s transformer architecture, which supports parallel operations. Unlike in the LSTM model, full poisoning with only mâ€‹lâ€‹p.câ€‹_â€‹fâ€‹cformulae-sequenceğ‘šğ‘™ğ‘ğ‘_ğ‘“ğ‘mlp.c\_fc layer (without top-k%percentğ‘˜k\% gradient masking) was found to be the most durable approach for GPT-2. This method reduces the likelihood of detection and ensures the backdoor persists longer, as the large-scale parameters make it less likely to be overwritten by benign clients.

VII Conclusion

We proposed a novel backdoor attack method called SDBA, a stealthy and long-lasting durable backdoor attack, particularly for NLP tasks. Through a comprehensive layer-wise analysis, SDBA identified the layers most vulnerable to backdoor injection, satisfying both stealth and durability requirements. SDBA ensures the attack remains stealthy and capable of evading even six representative defense mechanisms. Moreover, by applying top-kğ‘˜k% gradient masking within the specific layers, we significantly enhance the durability of the backdoor. Our experiments demonstrated that SDBA not only surpasses existing backdoor mechanisms but also exhibits stronger durability compared to recent backdoors previously regarded as highly durable.
Future work should focus on developing robust countermeasures to combat such stealthy and durable backdoor attacks. Also, from a defense perspective, our findings reveal significant threats to FL systems.
Additionally, as mentioned in this paper, large models, such as LLMs, do not utilize distance-based metrics like those used in Multi-Krum and FLAME due to the efficiency constraints of these defense methods. In summary, there is a growing need to counter increasingly stealthy and durable attacks, alongside the challenge of developing efficient defenses suitable for large models like LLMs. This highlights the need for more powerful and efficient defenses, not only for NLP tasks but also for LLMs.
