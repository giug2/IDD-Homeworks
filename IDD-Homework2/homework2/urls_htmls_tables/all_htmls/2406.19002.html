Abstract

To enhance straggler resilience in federated learning (FL) systems, a semi-decentralized approach has been recently proposed, enabling collaboration between clients. Unlike the existing semi-decentralized schemes, which adaptively adjust the collaboration weight according to the network topology, this letter proposes a deterministic coded network that leverages wireless diversity for semi-decentralized FL without requiring prior information about the entire network. Furthermore, the theoretical analyses of the outage and the convergence rate of the proposed scheme are provided. Finally, the superiority of our proposed method over benchmark methods is demonstrated through comprehensive simulations.

Index Terms:
Semi-decentralized federated learning, wireless network, diversity network code, communication stragglers

I Introduction

Federated learning (FL) is a promising distributed edge learning paradigm that leverages the computational capabilities of edge devices to exploit datasets localized on multiple edge clients, by iteratively optimizing a common objective function and collaborating with the central parameter server (PS) to learn a global model [mcmahan2017communication]. FL tackles two main challenges in edge learning: reducing communication overhead and preserving data privacy, by avoiding raw dataset sharing[yang2022federated]. FL keeps training data locally, thereby greatly decreasing the volume of transmitted data during the learning process. However, significant challenges arise within FL from its heterogeneous nature. Specifically, the non-i.i.d. (independently and identically distributed) data stored locally on a subset of clients does not represent the overall population distribution, consequently, partial client participation is highly likely to degrade FL performance [zhu2021federated].

Most existing works on FL with data dissimilarity consider error-free links under the underlying assumption that wireless imperfections can be handled by communication protocols[9014530, 9261995]. However, in practical FL scenarios where the clients and PS communicate over unreliable communication links, clients may fail to connect with PS due to communication resource constraints and thus cannot update their latest trained local models regularly. Such clients are known as communication stragglers. Partial client participation caused by communication stragglers can significantly impair FL performance. Therefore, a separate design between the communication system and learning process can be strictly sub-optimal[gunduz2020communicate].

I-1 Related works

There are multiple strategies to improve the straggler resilience in FL. The diverse physical environments may induce different possibilities of clients being stragglers. The impact of this different connectivity on FL convergence is investigated in [wang2021quantized], which proposes an adaptive resource allocation method to eliminate the objective inconsistency induced by stragglers and thus shrink the optimality gap. [vu2021straggler] designs client sampling strategies to reduce the probability of sampling a straggler in wireless FL with intermittent client availability and enhance FL convergence performance.

[yemini2022semi] proposes to alter the network topology to address stragglers by enabling communication between clients, rather than relying on sole communication between clients and PS, referred as semi-decentralized FL, or collaborative FL. However, the unbiased estimation of the true global model at PS in the aforementioned methods[wang2021quantized, vu2021straggler, yemini2022semi] is contingent upon precise prior information, such as entire network connectivity, client availability, and time synchronization, which markedly amplifies implementation complexity in real-world scenarios.

I-2 Our contributions

To overcome the limitations of the existing methods, we propose a novel deterministic cooperative network to address communication stragglers in FL. The proposed scheme does not necessitate prior information about the network and enables the retrieval of local models from a subset of clients. Our contributions are summarised as follows.

  • â€¢

    We propose a deterministic coded cooperative networking scheme for semi-decentralized FL based on the maximum distance separable (MDS) code exploiting wireless diversity. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to exploit coded diversity to mitigate stragglers in FL.

  • â€¢

    We conduct rigorous theoretical analyses of the proposed scheme. Specifically, we provide the outage analysis of PS not being able to see local model updates from each client through the intermittent network and the convergence rate analysis of the proposed scheme.

  • â€¢

    We verify the effectiveness of the proposed scheme by simulations and comparisons with benchmark methods.
