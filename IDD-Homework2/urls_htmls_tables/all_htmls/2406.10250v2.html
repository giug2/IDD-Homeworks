Abstract

This paper is concerned with portfolio optimization models for creating high-quality lists of recommended items to balance the accuracy and diversity of recommendations. However, the statistics (i.e., expectation and covariance of ratings) required for meanâ€“variance portfolio optimization are subject to inevitable estimation errors. To remedy this situation, we focus on robust optimization techniques that derive reliable solutions to uncertain optimization problems. Specifically, we propose a robust portfolio optimization model that copes with the uncertainty of estimated statistics based on the cardinality-based uncertainty sets. This robust portfolio optimization model can be reduced to a mixed-integer linear optimization problem, which can be solved exactly using mathematical optimization solvers. Experimental results using two publicly available rating datasets demonstrate that our method can improve not only the recommendation accuracy but also the diversity of recommendations compared with conventional meanâ€“variance portfolio optimization models. Notably, our method has the potential to improve the recommendation quality of various rating prediction algorithms.

1 Introduction

1.1 Background

Recent advances in information and communication technologies have enabled consumers to browse and purchase a wide variety of items through online services. Meanwhile, consumers often struggle to find items that match their preferences among a plethora of options. To overcome this information overload problem, recommender systemsÂ [1] have been widely implemented in online services. These systems generate personalized lists of unknown but potentially preferred items. In fact, integrating these systems into online platforms simplifies the search process and improves user experienceÂ [24].

Much of the research to date has focused on improving the prediction accuracy of recommender systemsÂ [4, 17]. To this end, a variety of rating prediction algorithms have been proposed, ranging from traditional methods such as collaborative filteringÂ [29] and matrix factorizationÂ [21] to recent deep learning techniquesÂ [11, 39].

In contrast, it has been pointed out that the most accurate recommendations do not always satisfy usersÂ [25]. Specifically, favorable recommendations must take into account the diversity and novelty of items provided for usersÂ [7, 18]. However, most recommender systems tend to offer popular items, leading to reduced diversity. Increasing the diversity and novelty of recommendations remains a major challenge in the advancement of recommender systems.

1.2 Related work

Prior studies have developed various algorithms to improve recommendation diversityÂ [7, 22, 26]. In particular, we explore applying financial portfolio theoryÂ [10] to recommender systems, aiming to balance the accuracy and diversity of recommendations.

Meanâ€“variance portfolio analysis has been applied to ranking systems in information retrieval and collaborative filteringÂ [32, 33]. Shi et al.Â [28] improved this ranking strategy by using the latent factor model for user ratings. KwonÂ [23] focused on the variance of ratings to increase the precision of top-Nğ‘Nitalic_N recommendations. Zhang and HurleyÂ [38] and Hurley and ZhangÂ [14] formulated several optimization models for selecting a list of diverse items. Xiao et al.Â [35] used a portfolio optimization model to recommend relevant services based on service risk facets. Yasumoto and TakanoÂ [37] developed shrinkage estimation methods to improve the accuracy of estimating the rating covariance matrix.

These studies have shown that the quality of recommendation lists can be improved by applying portfolio optimization. However, the statistics (i.e., expectation and covariance of ratings) required for meanâ€“variance portfolio optimization are subject to inevitable estimation errors. In fact, it has been pointed out that the meanâ€“variance portfolio optimization model often performs badly because of such estimation errorsÂ [6].

To remedy this situation, we focus on robust optimization techniquesÂ [2, 19] that derive reliable solutions to uncertain optimization problems. To the best of our knowledge, none of the prior studies have considered recommender systems that incorporate uncertainty of estimated statistics to select high-quality lists of recommendations.

1.3 Our contribution

The goal of this paper is to improve the recommendation quality of portfolio optimization models by using robust optimization techniques. Specifically, we propose a robust portfolio optimization model that copes with the uncertainty of estimated statistics based on the cardinality-based uncertainty setsÂ [3]. Our robust portfolio optimization model can be reduced to a mixed-integer linear optimization problem, which can be solved exactly using mathematical optimization solvers.

To verify the effectiveness of our method for recommendation, we conducted numerical experiments using two publicly available rating datasets. Experimental results demonstrate that our method can improve not only the recommendation accuracy but also the diversity of recommendations compared with conventional meanâ€“variance portfolio optimization models. This emphasizes that by properly handling the uncertainty in the expectation and covariance of user ratings, we can generate high-quality lists of recommendations that fulfill usersâ€™ diverse requirements. Moreover, our method has the potential to improve the recommendation quality of various rating prediction algorithms.

To evaluate both the accuracy and diversity of recommendations, we used the following evaluation metricsÂ [27]:
