Abstract

To achieve non-parametric NMT domain adaptation, k𝑘kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Machine Translation (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT) constructs an external datastore to store domain-specific translation knowledge, which derives a k𝑘kitalic_kNN distribution to interpolate the prediction distribution of the NMT model via a linear interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ. Despite its success, k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval at each timestep leads to substantial time overhead. To address this issue, dominant studies resort to k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with adaptive retrieval (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR), which dynamically estimates λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ and skips k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval if λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ is less than a fixed threshold. Unfortunately, k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR does not yield satisfactory results. In this paper, we first conduct a preliminary study to reveal two key limitations of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR: 1) the optimization gap leads to inaccurate estimation of λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ for determining k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping, and 2) using a fixed threshold fails to accommodate the dynamic demands for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval at different timesteps. To mitigate these limitations, we then propose k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with dynamic retrieval (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-DR) that significantly extends vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT in two aspects. Firstly, we equip k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with a MLP-based classifier for determining whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval at each timestep. Particularly, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features to fully exert the potential of the classifier. Secondly, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to dynamically generate the threshold, which further improves the efficiency of our model. Experimental results on the widely-used datasets demonstrate the effectiveness and generality of our model.111Our code is available at https://github.com/DeepLearnXMU/knn-mt-dr.

1 Introduction

As an effective paradigm for non-parametric domain adaptation, k𝑘kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Machine Translation (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT) (Khandelwal et al., 2020) derives from k𝑘kitalic_k-Nearest-Neighbor Language Model (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-LM) (Khandelwal et al., 2019) and has garnered much attention recently (Zheng et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022; Cao et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023b). Typically, k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT introduces translation knowledge stored in an external datastore to enhance the NMT model, which can conveniently achieve non-parametric domain adaptation by changing external datastores.

In k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, a datastore containing key-value pairs is first constructed with an off-the-shelf NMT model, where the key is the decoder representation and the value corresponds to its target token. During translation, the current decoder representation is used as a query to retrieve k𝑘kitalic_k nearest pairs from the datastore, where retrieved values are converted into a probability distribution. Finally, via a linear interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, this distribution is used to adjust the prediction distribution of the NMT model. In spite of success, retrieving at each timestep incurs substantial time overhead, which becomes considerable as the datastore expands.

To address this drawback, researchers have proposed two categories of approaches: 1) datastore compression that improves retrieval efficiency by reducing the size of datastores (Martins et al., 2022a; Meng et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022; Dai et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023a; Deguchi et al., 2023); 2) retrieval reduction that skips some k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval to speed up decoding. In this regard, the most representative work is k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with adaptive retrieval (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR) (Martins et al., 2022a) that skips k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval when the coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ is less than a fixed threshold α𝛼\alphaitalic_α. However, k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR does not achieve desired results as reported in (Martins et al., 2022a).

In this work, we mainly focus on the studies of retrieval reduction, which is compatible with the other type of studies. To this end, we first re-implement k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR  (Martins et al., 2022a) and conduct a preliminary study to analyze its limitations. Through in-depth analyses, we show that 1) the optimization gap leads to inaccurate estimation of λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ for determining k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping; 2) with the increase in timesteps, the demand for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes, which proves challenging for the fixed threshold α𝛼\alphaitalic_α to handle effectively.

To overcome the above defects, we then significantly extend the vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT into k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with dynamic retrieval (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-DR), which accelerates the model decoding in two aspects. Concretely, instead of relying on the interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, we introduce a MLP-based classifier to explicitly determine whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval as a binary classification task. Particularly, instead of using the decoder representation as the input of the classifier, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features to fully exert the potential of the classifier. Besides, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to dynamically generate the threshold, so as to further improve the efficiency of our model.

To summarize, main contributions of our work include the following four aspects:

  • •

    Through in-depth analyses, we conclude two defects of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR: the optimization gap leads to inaccurate estimation of λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping, and a fixed threshold is unable to effectively handle the varying demands of k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval at different timesteps.

  • •

    We propose to equip k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with an explicit classifier to determine whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval, where carefully-designed features enable our model to achieve a better balance between model acceleration and performance.

  • •

    We propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to further improve the efficiency of our model.

  • •

    Empirical evaluations on the multi-domain datasets validate the effectiveness of our model, as well as its compatibility with datastore compression methods.

2 Related Work

Datastore Compression.

In this aspect, the size of the datastore for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval is decreased to make retrieval efficient. For example,  Martins et al. (2022a) compress the datastore by greedily merging neighboring pairs that share the same values, and applying PCA algorithm (Wold et al., 1987) to reduce the dimension of stored keys. Meanwhile, Zhu et al. (2023a) prune the datastore based on the concept of local correctness, while Wang et al. (2022) presents a cluster-based compact network to condense the dimension of stored keys, coupled with a cluster-based pruning strategy to discard redundant pairs. Additionally, some studies opt for dynamically adopting more compact datastores. For instance, for each token in the input sentence, Meng et al. (2022) identify the relevant parallel sentences that contain this token and then collect corresponding word-aligned target tokens to construct a smaller datastore. Subsequently, Dai et al. (2023) conduct sentence-level retrieval and dynamically construct a compact datastore for each input sentence. With the same motivation, Deguchi et al. (2023) suggest retrieving target tokens from a subset of neighbor sentences related to the input sentence, where a look-up table based distance computation method is used to expedite retrieval.

Retrieval Reduction.

In this regard, some k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval is reduced to decrease time overhead for retrieval. For instance,  Martins et al. (2022b) adopt chunk-wise k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval rather than timestep-wise one, and Martins et al. (2022a) explore two approaches to reduce the frequency of k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval operations: 1) one introduces a caching mechanism to speed up decoding, where the cache mainly contains retrieved pairs from previous timesteps, and skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval if the distance between the query and any cached key is less than a predefined threshold; 2) the other proposes to conduct k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval when the interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ is less than a predefined threshold α𝛼\alphaitalic_α, which, however, does not achieve satisfactory results.

Our work mainly focuses on the second type of studies mentioned above. We first conduct a preliminary study to in-depth analyze two limitations of the λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ-based k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. To address these limitations, we introduce a classifier to explicitly determine whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval as a classification task. Notably, almost concurrently with our work, Shi et al. (2023) also use a classifier to speed up model decoding, sharing a similar motivation with ours. However, our work not only achieves better results, but also significantly differs from theirs in the following three aspects:

First, we explore several carefully-designed scalar features as the input for the classifier, which are crucial for achieving better performance. Second, when training the classifier, we adopt more reasonable criteria to construct training samples. To be specific, in addition to skipping retrieval when the target token ranks the 1111st position in the NMT prediction distribution, we believe that the model should also skip when the target token can not be obtained through k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval. Finally, based on the observation that the demand for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes as timesteps increase, we propose a timestep-aware threshold method to further improve the efficiency of our model.

k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with Adaptive Retrieval

Obviously, the retrieval of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT at each timestep incurs significant time overhead. To address this limitation, Martins et al. (2022a) follow He et al. (2021) to explore k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with adaptive retrieval (k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR). Unlike the vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, they dynamically estimate the interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ using a light MLP network, which takes several neural and count-based features as the input. Then, they not only interpolate the k𝑘kitalic_kNN and NMT prediction distributions with λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, but also skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval when λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ is less than a fixed threshold α𝛼\alphaitalic_α. During training, they minimize the cross-entropy (CE) loss over the interpolated translation distribution.

Unfortunately, extensive results on several commonly-used datasets indicate that k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR does not achieve satisfactory results.

3.2 Limitations of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR.

In this subsection, we conduct a preliminary study to explore the limitations of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR. We strictly follow the settings of (Martins et al., 2022a) to re-implement their k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR, and then conduct two groups of experiments on the commonly-used multi-domain datasets released by Aharoni and Goldberg (2020).

As reported by Martins et al. (2022a), dynamically determining whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval based on λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ leads to significant performance degradation. In the first group of experiments, to further provide evidence of this conclusion, we perform decoding on the test sets in a teacher-forcing manner and analyze the F1 scores of λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ-based k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. As shown in Table 1, F1 scores remain relatively low no matter which thresholds and datasets are used.

For the above results, we believe that there are two reasons leading to the inaccurate estimation of λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, which in turn makes λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ unsuitable for deciding whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval.

In addition to lacking the information of k𝑘kitalic_kNN distribution for λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ estimation222Due to the consideration of model efficiency, k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR do not exploit the k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval information to estimate λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, which has been shown to be effective in previous studies (Zheng et al., 2021; Jiang et al., 2022)., we believe that the optimization objective of minimizing the CE loss over the translation distribution may be unsuitable to train an accurate 𝝀𝝀\bm{\lambda}bold_italic_λ estimator for determining k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. To verify this claim, we consider whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval as a standard binary classification task and use a binary CE loss to train a classifier for λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ estimation. Note that this classifier is also based on MLP and contains the same input as k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR. To avoid description confusion, we denote the λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ trained by k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR and the above binary CE loss as Tran-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ and Bina-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ, respectively. Then, we calculate the average absolute value of the difference between Bina-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ and Tran-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ at all timesteps. The statistical results show that the average difference is 0.14950.14950.14950.1495, and 29.1229.1229.1229.12% of timesteps exhibit a difference exceeding 0.20.20.20.2. These findings indicate significant differences between Bina-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ and Tran-λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ.

In the second group of experiments, we conduct experiments with vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT on the validation sets to explore the impact of k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval during different timestep intervals. Specifically, we limit the model to only perform k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval in specific timestep intervals, where each interval starts from 00 and increases by 5555 timesteps in length, and we only use instances with a translation length no less than the interval’s right endpoint. From Figure 1, we observe that with the increase in timesteps, the performance gain caused by k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval gradually decreases across all datasets. This observation reveals that the demand for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval varies at different timesteps, which can not be handled well by the fixed threshold 𝜶𝜶\bm{\alpha}bold_italic_α in k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR.

In summary, the above two defects seriously limit the practicality of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR. Therefore, it is of great significance to explore more effective skipping k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval methods for k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT.

4 Our Model

In this section, we significantly extend k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT into k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-DR in the following two aspects.

4.1 Classifier for Determining k𝑘kitalic_kNN Retrieval Skipping

Unlike k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR leveraging the interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ for determining whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval, we directly equip k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT with a binary classifier to determine whether to skip at each timestep. This classifier comprises a two-layer MLP network with ReLU activation. At timestep t𝑡titalic_t, we conduct k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval only if the prediction probability of the classifier on conducting k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval exceeds a timestep-aware threshold αtsubscript𝛼𝑡\alpha_{t}italic_α start_POSTSUBSCRIPT italic_t end_POSTSUBSCRIPT, otherwise we will directly skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval. In the following, we will discuss the classifier, which involves the construction of training samples, input features, and the training objective.

5 Experiments

5.1 Setup

Datasets.

We conduct experiments on the multi-domains dataset released by Aharoni and Goldberg (2020). The dataset comprises German-English parallel corpora across five domains: Koran, IT, Medical, Law, and Subtitles, the detailed statistics can be found in Appendix A. We employ Byte Pair Encoding (Sennrich et al., 2016) to split words into subwords. Finally, we use two metrics to evaluate the translation quality: SacreBLEU444https://github.com/mjpost/sacrebleu (Post, 2018) and COMET555https://github.com/unbabel/COMET (Rei et al., 2020).

Model Configuration.

We develop our model with k𝑘kitalic_kNN-BOX666https://github.com/NJUNLP/knn-box (Zhu et al., 2023c) and use Faiss (Johnson et al., 2019) to build the datastore and search nearest neighbors. To ensure fair comparisons, we adopt the same settings as the previous study (Khandelwal et al., 2020). Concretely, we set the number of retrieved pairs to 8, the temperature τ𝜏\tauitalic_τ to 100100100100 for Koran and 10101010 for the other datasets, and λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ to 0.70.70.70.7 for IT, Subtitles, 0.80.80.80.8 for the other datasets. We use a two-layer MLP network with ReLU activation (Agarap, 2018) to construct our classifier, of which hidden size is set to 32323232 because it is not sensitive in our model. Besides, we set the hyper-parameter αminsubscript𝛼min\alpha_{\text{min}}italic_α start_POSTSUBSCRIPT min end_POSTSUBSCRIPT to 0.450.450.450.45 for Koran, Subtitles, 0.40.40.40.4 for the other datasets.777The details of tuning αminsubscript𝛼min\alpha_{\text{min}}italic_α start_POSTSUBSCRIPT min end_POSTSUBSCRIPT are reported in Appendix C.

Baselines.

Our baselines include:

  • •

    Base NMT (Ng et al., 2019). Following Khandelwal et al. (2020), we use the WMT’19 German-English news translation task winner as the base NMT model.

  • •

    Vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT (Khandelwal et al., 2020). It serves as a baseline, upon which we develop our model.

  • •

    k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR  (Martins et al., 2022a). It performs retrieval only when the interpolation coefficient λ𝜆\lambdaitalic_λ is less than a predefined threshold α𝛼\alphaitalic_α. Note that it is our most important baseline. Particularly, we report the performance of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR with α𝛼\alphaitalic_α set to 0.250.250.250.25, 0.500.500.500.50, and 0.750.750.750.75, respectively.

  • •

    Faster k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT (Shi et al., 2023). It is a concurrent work with ours, where a two-layer MLP network takes decoder representation as the input to determine whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval at each timestep.

  • •

    SK-MT (Dai et al., 2023). It dynamically constructs a compact datastore by conducting sentence-level retrieval for each input sentence. Specially, we report the performance of SK-MT1 with m=2𝑚2m=2italic_m = 2, k=1𝑘1k=1italic_k = 1 and SK-MT2 with m=16𝑚16m=16italic_m = 16, k=2𝑘2k=2italic_k = 2.

5.2 Main Results

To comprehensively evaluate various models, we report their translation quality and decoding speed.

Translation Quality.

Table 2 presents BLEU and COMET scores of various models on the multi-domain test sets. We observe that both k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR and Faster k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT suffer from significant performance declines compared to Vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, echoing with the results reported in previous studies (Martins et al., 2022a; Shi et al., 2023). In contrast, our model exhibits the least performance degradation. Specifically, our model achieves average BLEU and COMET scores of 41.8341.8341.8341.83 and 81.7881.7881.7881.78 points, with only 0.740.740.740.74 and 0.180.180.180.18 points lower than those of Vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, respectively. Although SK-MT2 performs better than our model, experiments in Section 5.4 find that it is not compatible with Adaptive k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, while our model significantly outperforms SK-MT2 when using Adaptive k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT as the base model.

Decoding Speed.

Model efficiency is a crucial performance indicator for k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT. As implemented in previous studies (Zheng et al., 2021; Deguchi et al., 2023), we try different batch sizes: 1111, 16161616, 32323232, 64646464 and 128128128128, and then report the model efficiency using “#Tok/Sec”: the number of translation tokens generated by the model per second.

Experimental results are listed in Table 3. We have the following interesting findings: First, regardless of the batch size used, our model is more efficient than both Vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT, k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR(α𝛼\alphaitalic_α=0.250.250.250.25), SK-MT1 and SK-MT2.

Second, as the batch size increases, the efficiency advantage of our model becomes more apparent. On most datasets, we find that the acceleration ratios of our model with large batch sizes (64 or 128) are significantly higher than those with small batch sizes (1 or 16). Finally, with the increase of the datastore size, the efficiency advantage of our model also becomes more significant. As analysed in Appendix A, the datastore in Subtitles contains the maximum number of pairs while the datastore in Koran is the smallest. Correspondingly, our model has the most significant acceleration effect on the Subtitles dataset, while the acceleration effect on the Koran dataset is the least significant.

Based on the above experimental results, we believe that compared with baselines, ours can achieve better balance between model performance degradation and acceleration.

6 Conclusion and Future Work

In this work, we first in-depth analyze the limitations of k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-AR, and then significantly extend the vanilla k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT to k𝑘kitalic_kNN-MT-DR in two aspects. First, we equip the model with a classifier to determine whether to skip k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval, where several carefully-designed scalar features are exploited to exert the potential of the classifier. Second, we propose a timestep-aware threshold adjustment method to further refine k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval skipping. Extensive experiments and analyses verify the effectiveness of our model.

Inspired by (Li et al., 2023), we will further improve our model by incorporating more source-side information into our classifier. Besides, we aim to generalize our model to k𝑘kitalic_kNN-LM (Khandelwal et al., 2019) and multilingual scenario (Stap and Monz, 2023), so as to validate its generalizability.

Limitations

As our model integrates an additional classifier, there is an associated increase in time consumption. Notably, as the size of the datastore decreases, the time overhead for k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval diminishes and classifier-related time cost becomes more apparent, which results in a less pronounced acceleration in decoding. Besides, the experiments of decoding speed are evaluated solely on a single computer, while the time overhead of k𝑘kitalic_kNN retrieval may differ across different hardware, yielding varied acceleration results.
