Abstract

We consider a novel algorithm, for the completion of partially observed low-rank matrices in a structured setting where each entry can be chosen from a finite discrete alphabet set, such as in common recommender systems. The proposed low-rank matrix completion (MC) method is an improved variation of state-of-the-art (SotA) discrete aware matrix completion method which we previously proposed, in which discreteness is enforced by an ℓ0subscriptℓ0\ell_{0}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 0 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm regularizer, not by replaced with the ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 1 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm, but instead approximated by a continuous and differentiable function normalized via fractional programming (FP) under a proximal gradient (PG) framework. Simulation results demonstrate the superior performance of the new method compared to the SotA techniques as well as the earlier ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 1 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm-based discrete-aware matrix completion approach.

I Introduction

With the recent popularity of machine learning and big data, matrix completion has become a problem in many modern applications stemming from different areas, such as recommendation systems in computer science [1], localization algorithms in signal processing [2], and millimeter wave channel estimation in wireless communications [3].

The most common problems are often structured low-rank MC problems, where 𝑿∈ℝm×n𝑿superscriptℝ𝑚𝑛\boldsymbol{X}\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}bold_italic_X ∈ blackboard_R start_POSTSUPERSCRIPT italic_m × italic_n end_POSTSUPERSCRIPT is the desired low-rank matrix obtained from the partially observed, incomplete matrix 𝑶∈ℝm×n𝑶superscriptℝ𝑚𝑛\boldsymbol{O}\in\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}bold_italic_O ∈ blackboard_R start_POSTSUPERSCRIPT italic_m × italic_n end_POSTSUPERSCRIPT [4, 5, 6], and traditional MC solutions [7, 8, 9] mainly seek to improve performance and or lower complexity by replacing the non-convex rank objective with corresponding convex alternatives, i.e. the nuclear norm (NN).

To cite a few SotA examples, an MC approach employing the NN as a bound on the rank objective was proposed in [7], which was shown to be able to fill matrices even with a few noisy observations. Another approach proposed in [8] describes the relaxed minimization of the NN, solved via semidefinite programming (SDP). Since the complexity of the SDP solution is of order 𝑶⁢(max⁢(m,n)4)𝑶maxsuperscript𝑚𝑛4\boldsymbol{O}(\text{max}(m,n)^{4})bold_italic_O ( max ( italic_m , italic_n ) start_POSTSUPERSCRIPT 4 end_POSTSUPERSCRIPT ), where m𝑚mitalic_m and n𝑛nitalic_n are the dimensions of the matrix, the method can only solve the problem up to a certain size before becoming unfeasible. In order to lower the complexity of the problem, the technique proposed in [10] relies on singular value thresholding (SVT) as a PG minimizer of the NN function.

Motivated by that idea, we recently proposed in [11] a similar PG-based low-complexity low-rank MC method to solve a harder variation of the problem faced when the matrix entries must belong to a finite discrete alphabet. The resulting discrete-aware MC technique is especially efficient to solve problems such as in recommendation and rating applications [1]. This discreteness in the previously proposed method is included by adding a discrete-space regularizer in the optimization problem, with the solution offered in closed-form under a relaxed variation in which the regularizer is convexized by using the ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 1 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm.

In this article, we propose an improvement of the latter discrete-aware matrix completion algorithm [11] in which the discrete-space regularizer is the actual ℓ0subscriptℓ0\ell_{0}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 0 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm, relaxed by first replacing it with an arbitrarily-tight continuous and differentiable, but not convex, function, which is then convexized via FP. The method also makes use a PG algorithm designed specifically for the formulated problem.

The structure of the article is as follows. First, basics on the MC problem, as well as a review of the discrete-aware variation of the MC proposed in [11] is given in Section II. Then, the new method based on the ℓ0subscriptℓ0\ell_{0}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 0 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm approximation is described in Section III, followed by simulations results comparing our contribution with SotA methods and a few concluding remarks, in Sections IV and V, respectively.

V Conclusion

We proposes a new discrete-aware MC algorithm in which a discrete-space ℓ0subscriptℓ0\ell_{0}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 0 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm regularizer is introduced to the objective function, which is later approximated with asymptotically tight precision. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed scheme outperforms both SotA techniques, as well as our earlier discrete aware method using a regularizer relaxed by the ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1}roman_ℓ start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 1 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT-norm.
