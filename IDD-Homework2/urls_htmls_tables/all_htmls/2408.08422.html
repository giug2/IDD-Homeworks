3 The Rare Disease Question Answering (ReDis-QA) Dataset

In this section, we introduce the Rare Disease Question-and-Answer (ReDis-QA) dataset in details. Specifically, the ReDis-QA dataset aims to provide a comprehensive resource for healthcare researchers, practitioners, and enthusiasts to explore the intricacies of rare diseases. The data instances of ReDis-QA dataset are sourced from the online open-source database of the National Organization for Rare Disorders***https://rarediseases.org/.

3.1    Dataset Overview

ReDis-QA comprehensively collects data from 1324 rare diseases. For each rare disease, the questions are collected from seven aspects, including the overview, symptoms, causes, affects, related disorders, diagnosis, and standard therapies of the disease. ReDis-QA provides the golden answers for these questions. In this way, ReDis-QA has 9268 QA pairs in total. The overview of the ReDis-QA dataset is given in Figure 1.

ReDis-QA has all QA paris in natural language, for training and evaluating LLMs. For instance, for the Alpha Thalassemia disease, the questions are: “What is the Alpha Thalassemia?”; “What are the symptoms of Alpha Thalassemia?”; “What may cause Alpha Thalassemia?”; “What may Alpha Thalassemia affect?”; “What are the related disorders of Alpha Thalassemia?”; “How to diagnose the Alpha Thalassemia?; and How to treat the Alpha Thalassemia? The golden answer to these questions are in shown in Figure 2.

3.2    Rare Disease Dignosing Tasks

The ReDis-QA dataset

Disease Encyclopedia

This task evalutes the performance of LLMs as an encyclopedia of rare diseases, seeking to discover their reliability as a information source of rare diseases, or identify areas requiring further reinforcement. In this task, LLMs generate answers for all the questions in the ReDis-QA dataset. The answers are compared with the well-established information about rare diseases, which are the golden answers given by the dataset.

Disease Analysis.

This task evalutes LLMs in terms of analyzing the symptoms and affects of a disease, given its overview and causes. Specifically, the objective is to evaluate the capacity of LLMs in leveraging subtle hints within disease overviews and underlying causes to draw conclusions regarding the potential outcomes of the disease. In the broader context of the healthcare domain, a profound understanding of disease symptoms and affects is crucial for early detection and timely medical treatment.

Disease Treatment.

This task assesses the capacity of LLM in diagnosing and treating a disease, given its overview, causes, symptoms, and affects.

3.3    Evaluation Metrics

BERTScore.

The BERTScore adopts the pre-trained contextual embeddings from BERT, aligning words in candidate and reference sentences to the Euclidean space. Subsequently, it employs cosine similarity for evaluation. The BERTScore has demonstrated a correlation with human judgment on sentence-level and system-level evaluation.

ROUGE.

BELU.

The Bilingual Evaluation Understudy (BLEU) is designed to assess the quality of machine algorithm outputs by measuring their correspondence with the reference answers. Specifically, the BLUE scores are computed for individual translated segments, which can be either words or phrases. This calculation involves comparing the translated segments with the golden reference. Subsequently, these individual scores are averaged over the entire corpus to indicate the overall quality. The BLUE score does not take the smoothness or grammatical correctness into account.
