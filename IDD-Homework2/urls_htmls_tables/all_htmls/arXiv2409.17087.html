We wish to submit a new manuscript entitled “Quanv4EO: Empowering Earth Observation by means of Quanvolutional Neural Networks”.

In recent years, Machine Learning (ML) algorithms have become widespread in all fields of Earth Observation (EO) helping to hugely improve data processing. However, the adoption of ML techniques has not completely solved this issue since the largest portion of the processing time has been moved to the pre-processing steps, such as the training phase of the ML model. To tackle these challenges, new and innovative computational technologies based on Quantum Computing (QC) are going to play a very important role in this field. Indeed, quantum computers are showing a large potential to efficiently solve important problems handled with difficulty on traditional computers. Therefore, QC and ML have started to be jointly considered in many EO-related applications. This is also confirmed by the increasing interest of big players in this sector, for instance, the Oak Ridge National Laboratory in U.S.A.111 ORNL Quantum Computing Institute: https://quantum.ornl.gov/, the European Space Agency, DLR, Thales222QC4EO study: https://eo4society.esa.int/projects/qc4eo-study/, QA4EO study: https://eo4society.esa.int/projects/qa4eo-study/ as well as our IEEE GRSS: https://www.grss-ieee.org/community/groups-initiatives/high-performance-and-disruptive-computing-in-remote-sensing-hdcrs/.

To the best of our knowledge, a limited number of works applying quantum machine learning (QML) to EO tasks appear in the current state of the art, and none of them explore the use of quanvolutional layers for EO tasks. In this manuscript, starting from the core idea proposed by Henderson et Al.333Henderson, M., Shakya, S., Pradhan, S. et al. Quanvolutional neural networks: powering image recognition with quantum circuits. Quantum Mach. Intell. 2, 2 (2020). https://doi.org/10.1007/s42484-020-00012-y, that demonstrate the goodness of quanvolution in the field of data science, we investigate, re-implement and tailor this methodology to various EO tasks.

This manuscript highlights a significant shift towards leveraging quantum computing techniques in processing large volumes of remote sensing data. The proposed Quanv4EO model, differently from the one of Henderson et Al., introduces a quanvolution method for preprocessing multi-dimensional EO data, efficiently and more robustly. The code has been developed from scratch and, if the reviewers are interested, we may provide the code immediately upon request. Qualitative and quantitative results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture and strategy. We really believe that our work can be of interest to and have a big impact for the EO community.
