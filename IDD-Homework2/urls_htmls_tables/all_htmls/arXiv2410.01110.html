Abstract

Few-shot medical image segmentation (FSMIS) aims to perform the limited annotated data learning in the medical image analysis scope. Despite the progress has been achieved, current FSMIS models are all trained and deployed on the same data domain, as is not consistent with the clinical reality that medical imaging data is always across different data domains (e.g. imaging modalities, institutions and equipment sequences). How to enhance the FSMIS models to generalize well across the different specific medical imaging domains? In this paper, we focus on the matching mechanism of the few-shot semantic segmentation models and introduce an Earth Mover‚Äôs Distance (EMD) calculation based domain robust matching mechanism for the cross-domain scenario. Specifically, we formulate the EMD transportation process between the foreground support-query features, the texture structure aware weights generation method, which proposes to perform the sobel based image gradient calculation over the nodes, is introduced in the EMD matching flow to restrain the domain relevant nodes. Besides, the point set level distance measurement metric is introduced to calculated the cost for the transportation from support set nodes to query set nodes. To evaluate the performance of our model, we conduct experiments on three scenarios (i.e., cross-modal, cross-sequence and cross-institution), which includes six medical datasets and involves three body regions, and the results demonstrate that our model achieves the SoTA performance against the compared models.

1 Introduction

Few-shot medical image segmentation (FSMIS) ¬†[24, 6, 30] is the potential solution for performing segmentation under the scenario of insufficient annotated data, legal and user privacy constraints. The aim of the FSMIS task is to perform generalized segmentation on the novel categories of targets using only a few annotated medical images. With recent years of development, there are two kinds of FSMIS technical schools including: the interactive network based methods [28, 4, 38] and prototype network based methods [24, 23, 51, 2, 6, 27]. However, with rethinking of the experimental settings conducted in these methods, we find that both of them are trained and deployed on the identical medical imaging, which is not coincident with the clinical reality that the medical imaging data tends to be collected from multiple domains (i.e., multi-modal, multi-sequence and multi-institution) [43, 22, 41, 47]. Therefore, for ensuring the generalization capability of the FSMIS model across different data domains, we introduce the task: Cross-domain Few-shot Medical Image Segmentation (CD-FSMIS) in this paper. Under the cross-domain scenario, we argue that the main challenge of CD-FSMIS task is to learn the inter-class general and domain agnostic semantic representations which enable the capability of transferring from support set to query set and defensing the shifts inter-domains at the same time. For learning of the domain agnostic representation, the previous single-source domain generalization (SDG) methods introduce to train the model on diverse distributions of source data obtained by different augmentation algorithms including causality-inspired augmentation [25], b√©zier curve based style augmentation [50], global location-scale augmentation [32] and adversarial learning based augmentation [1]. While in recent few-shot semantic segmentation (FSS) algorithms [36, 3, 20, 48, 7], most of them focus on conducting the matching or correlation relations between the support set information and the query set information by employing different variants of attention mechanisms [3, 26, 36, 39].

However, the existing matching mechanisms of FSMIS models may not adapt perfectly to the sharp discrepancy in the local characteristics and tiny textured structure of images under the cross-domain scenario, especially in the medical image analysis scope, which leads to the performance degradation of model in the test target data domain. Specifically, as shown in Fig. 1, when imaging the liver organ, more details of cavity inside the liver are presented in the CT scans, while the MRI-T2 images display more structures of water-contained blood vessels and soft tissue portions. This imaging discrepancy exists in the tiny structure of organs causes the matching imbalance between the support set information and query set information in previous FSMIS models during the model inference phase. Based on above analysis, we focus on the matching of few-shot learning models and introduce a novel matching mechanism which can resist the domain shifts. Therefore, we introduce the RobustEMD matching mechanism that based on the Earth Mover‚Äôs Distance (EMD) [29] between the support set features and the query set features. Different from the previous EMD based method DeepEMD [44] for the few-shot classification task, which does not take into account the influence of the shifts (e.g., image structure discrepancy) across different domains towards the matching accuracy. The targeted modification here for conventional EMD algorithm is the consideration of the cross-domain conditions. Different from the DeepEMD model, which aims to alleviate the intra-class variation of the few-shot learning and proposes the cross-reference mechanism to generate the weights of nodes, our work chooses to re-formulate the EMD into a domain robust matching mechanism which generates the correlative scores between the support feature and query feature.

Specifically, we first obtain the support foreground feature and the query foreground feature and then uniformly decompose them evenly into nùëõnitalic_n local features. Due to that some of channels in feature exhibit unstable activations across different domains [5], we propose to set the per channel-wise feature map as the node vector for formulating EMD matching flow. For weighting the each node, we argue that more complexity level of texture in node reflects the higher domain-relevant characteristic, which means we should assign the small weights to the nodes with high level of texture complexity. Therefore, our node weight generation method first captures the texture signals with calculation of bidirectional gradient values over the node and then evaluates the signal complexity by the local-variance metric. The activated local-variance values will act as the weights of node. After the node weights generation, the conventional EMD matching flow will directly employ the l1subscriptùëô1l_{1}italic_l start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 1 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT or l2subscriptùëô2l_{2}italic_l start_POSTSUBSCRIPT 2 end_POSTSUBSCRIPT norms and cosine similarity as the cost calculation metric for the node transportation unit. However, from the perspective of enhancing the generalization, we introduce the boundary aware hausdorff distance calculation and modify it as the node transportation cost function in EMD flow. With the solved least-expensive matching flow by linear programming, the EMD distances between foreground support local features and foreground query local features are calculated and activated for calibrating them into domain robust, which improves the cross-domain segmentation performance.

In conclusion, our contributions can be summarized as follows:

  • ‚Ä¢

2 Related Works

2.1 Few-shot Semantic Segmentation

To address the lack of annotated images in semantic segmentation, Few-shot Semantic Segmentation (FSS) task is introduced. Through several years of development, there are two main categories of existing FSS models including MatchingNet based methods and PrototypicalNet based methods. Among them, the MatchingNet based FSS methods aim to conduct the fast transferable matching mechanisms between support features and query features [3, 21] and establish a pixel-wise interaction mechanism between support set and query set [26]. The main idea of these MatchingNet based methods is to extract the efficient and dense representation correspondences from the support image annotations to query images, ultimately aiming to enhance the generalization capability of FSS model. Besides, the PrototypicalNet based FSS methods aim to learn the accurate and general semantic prototypical representations from the extracted support feature and query feature. Some of the cutting-edge FSS models propose to aggregate multiple prototypes from pixel-level to region-level for representing distinct semantic classes with the advanced technologies, such as clustering algorithm [42, 16], Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm [40] and transformer [46, 45]. Moreover, some researchers also reevaluate the FSS task from some novel perspectives, including learning extra knowledge from the non-target regions (background or boundaries) for boosting the ability of generalizing [19, 13, 14] and re-design the feature encoder for alleviating the heterogeneity phenomenon of the intra-class diversity between support image and query image [8].

2.2 Cross-domain Few-shot Segmentation

The cross-domain few-shot segmentation (CD-FSS) [15, HUANG2023102694, 35] is proposed to alleviate the performance degradation of FSS model while meeting with the unfamiliar domain images. The PATNet [15] proposes a pyramid-anchor transformation to transform the domain-specific features into the domain-agnostic features, and the RD [35] proposes a meta-memory bank to store the intra-domain style information for adaptation from the source domain to the target domain. Besides, RestNet [9] further improves the PATNet with introducing the novel residual transformation to map the original feature representations as the domain-agnostic. In this paper, we focus on the improving the metric based matching mechanism with the premise of ensuring the domain generalization ability in medical imaging scenarios, and introduce a Earth Mover‚Äôs Distance (EMD) calculation metric based matching mechanism with improved node weighting mechanism and node transferring cost function.

2.3 Earth Mover‚Äôs Distance

The Earth Mover‚Äôs Distance (EMD) originally performs as the solution to the transportation problem by using the linear programming algorithm, and its original application in computer vision is the image retrieval [49]. In the few-shot learning task, the DeepEMD [44] first designs the differentiable EMD metric for determining the structural representation distance between the support features and the query features. Moreover, the SADA [34] further employs the EMD metric to optimize the vision-language prototypes for aligning the distribution of each semantic class in the few-shot learning task. In this work, we re-design the node vectors formulation method and introduce a novel node weighting mechanism for cross-domain task, and also introduce the boundary reserving points set distance based function for the transportation cost calculation between the support nodes and the query nodes under the cross-domain scenario.

3.2 Model Overview

The main contribution of our work is to design a domain robust matching mechanism (i.e., RobustEMD) between the support foreground feature and the query foreground feature in cross-domain few-shot medical image segmentation task. As shown in the Fig. 2, on the whole, our proposed model contains three components including: (1) foreground features generation, (2) EMD based domain robust matching mechanism and (3) calibration and prediction. Among them, the EMD based domain robust matching mechanism is the key component which introduces a novel node weights generation method and a boundary aware transportation cost function. Specifically, with the obtained support-query foreground features, the matching mechanism calculates the correlation scores between support foreground features and query foreground features. Then, the subsequent calibration mechanism employs these scores to transform features from domain-sensitive into domain-invariant.

3.4 RobustEMD: EMD based Domain Robust Matching Mechanism

3.4.1 Motivation

The matching mechanism aims to formulate the generalizable interaction or correlation between the support image and the query image, which is the core of the metric based few-shot learning algorithms. Therefore, our work aims to design a domain robust matching mechanism based on the EMD matching flow. Different from the DeepEMD [44] which employs EMD as the distance metric to alleviate the intra-class variation problem for the few-shot classification, we introduce the domain robust EMD matching (RobustEMD) for transforming or calibrating features into domain agnostic, which aims to calculate the correlation scores between support image foreground feature and query image foreground feature. To specific, in the RobustEMD matching flow, the texture complexity aware EMD node weighting mechanism is introduced to de-emphasize these nodes with rich image texture signals which are considered as domain relevant characteristics during the EMD transportation procedure. 4 Experiments and Results

4.1 Experimental Configurations

4.1.1 Datasets

We conduct the evaluation of our proposed model performance on three categories of cross-domain scenarios including: cross-modal, cross-sequence and cross-institution. Specifically, the cross-modal dataset contains 20 cases of MRI modality images from the ISBI 2019 Combined Healthy Organ Segmentation challenge (CHAOS) [11] and 20 cases of CT modality images from the MICCAI 2015 multi-atlas labeling Beyond The Cranial Vault Challenge (BTCV) [12]. The cross-sequence dataset consists of 35 cases of Late Gadolinium Enhancement (LGE) sequence cardiac MRI images and 35 cases of balanced-Steady State Free Precession (bSSFP) sequence cardiac MRI images which are from the MICCAI 2019 Multi-sequence Cardiac MR Segmentation challenge (CMR) [52, 37], and the cross-institution dataset includes the prostate MRI images from the SmartTarget Biopsy Trial center and prostate MRI images from TCIA Prostate3T center which are provided by [17].

4.1.2 Implementation Details

For each cross-domain experiment, using the MRI‚ü∂‚ü∂\longrightarrow‚ü∂CT setting of cross-modal dataset as example, our model is trained on the abdominal MRI dataset CHAOS and then evaluated its generalization capability on the abdominal CT dataset BTCV, and the CT‚ü∂‚ü∂\longrightarrow‚ü∂MRI setting is vice versa. At first, we choose to reformat all of 3D scan images from datasets into 2D axial images and also resample them into the size of 256√ó256256256256\times 256256 √ó 256. And obtained single channel images are repeated to 3 channels images with size of 256√ó256√ó32562563256\times 256\times 3256 √ó 256 √ó 3 for suitable for the convolution input dimension. The self-supervised learning paradigm [24] for few-shot medical image segmentation is conducted during training, where the 3D supervoxel clustering algorithm by ADNet [6] is employed to generate the pseudo masks on all training images.

Besides, about the details of model implementation settings, we adopt the episode based meta-learning strategy [28] for support slices and query slices selection during model training and testing, and the corresponding 1 way 1 shot learning configuration is also formulated. For evaluating the model generalization performance, the five-fold cross-validation dataset partitioning method from ADNet [6] is also employed in the training and testing stages of model. 5 Conclusion and Discussion

In this work, we aim to design a domain robust matching mechanism for the cross-domain few-shot medical image segmentation task, and this matching mechanism is based on the EMD calculation, called RobustEMD. Specifically, we formulate the matching flow between the obtained support foreground feature and the query foreground feature, and choose to set each channel map of local representation as the node vector in the EMD matching flow. We introduce a novel node weighting method which aim to capture the texture signal complexity value over the node representation as the node weight. And the new node transportation cost function is also introduced to perform the boundary reserving node transportation. Finally, the correlation scores between support representations and query representations is calculated and employed to calibrate representations from domain relevant into domain agnostic. Experiments on three cross-domain datasets (cross-modal, cross-sequence and cross-institution) are conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of model under the cross-domain setting.

However, there are also existed some limitations which are necessary further exploration. First of all, we still employ the few-shot medical image segmentation pipeline of using the supervoxel clustering based self-supervised learning during the model training phase. The attempts for more accurate pseudo masks for segmentation or training model without the self-supervised learning should be considered in the subsequent works. Besides, more categories of domain relevant factors over the initial image level or the extracted inside features level are meaningful to explore in the further works.
