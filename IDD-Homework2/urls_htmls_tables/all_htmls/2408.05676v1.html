3 Literature Review

\thesubsection Reranking for Recommender Systems

Several existing works have demonstrated the effectiveness of re-ranking by modeling item mutual influences and interactions in the initial ranking lists [dlcm, prm, setrank, feng2021revisit]. For example, Ai \etal[dlcm] use a recurrent neural network to sequentially encode the top-ranked items with their feature vectors, and learn a local context model to re-rank the top results. Similarly, Zhuang \etal[zhuang2018globally] design a recurrent neural network with an attention mechanism to capture long-distance influences between items. Inspired by the Transformer architecture [vaswani2017attention] used in machine translation, Pei \etal[prm] propose to use the Transformer to model the mutual influences between items. The Transformer structure uses the self-attention mechanism where the relationship between any two items is reflected with an attention score. Pang \etal[setrank] study the permutation-invariant property of the self-attention blocks in re-ranking. Feng \etal[feng2021revisit] also investigate the permutation influence in re-ranking and generate candidate re-ranking permutations by beam search. Yet all of them overlook users’ diversified interests towards the re-ranked lists.

\thesubsection Diversity

In the context of search result diversification, explicit intent-aware diversity frameworks aim at promoting a hybrid of coverage over explicit aspects of a query and novelty. An early example of such kind of algorithm in information retrieval is the Maximum Marginal Relevance (MMR) re-ranking algorithm [carbonell1998use]. IA-Select [agrawal2009diversifying] maximizes the probability that the average user finds at least one useful result within the top-K𝐾Kitalic_K results. PM-2 [dang2012diversity] provides search results that best maintain the overall proportionality of documents belonging to different aspects. The intra-list similarity is calculated in [ziegler2005improving] to diversify the topics in the list. xQuAD [santos2010exploiting] is a probabilistic framework to balance relevance and novelty. However, result diversification in these information retrieval applications focuses on diversifying the search results over topic aspects and does not study users’ personal diversified preferences towards the re-ranked lists. \wwaddDPP NIPS’18
