Abstract:

In this proceeding contribution we report on the ongoing effort to understand and simulate Wilson twisted mass fermions in the so called Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime.

1 Introduction

Simulations in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime are complementary to standard large volume simulations. They allow to extract low energy constants of the chiral Lagrangian, in some cases with less contaminations from chiral logs coming from higher order corrections. For a long time it has been thought that simulations in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime are restricted to chirally invariant lattice formulations. In ref.Â [1] we have argued that actually this is not the case, and that simulations in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime can be performed also with non chirally invariant lattice actions such as Wilson like fermions.

In particular inÂ [1] we suggested that a combination of algorithmic and theoretical understanding of Wilson twisted mass makes it possible to actually perform simulations in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime with Wilson twisted mass fermions.

Recently it has been shown that with suitable and related algorithmic ideasÂ [2] it possible to reach or get close to the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime also with improved Wilson fermions. At this lattice conference further results in this directions have been presentedÂ [3].

2 Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon expansion

Lowering the quark mass at finite lattice spacing with Wilson-like fermions requires a detailed understanding of the interplay between the genuine chiral behaviour induced by the â€™pionâ€™ dynamics and the one generated by cutoff effects. A review on the phase diagram and cutoff effects with Wilson twisted mass (Wtm) can be found in ref.Â [7]. In the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime this is equivalent to saying that it is necessary to understand the coupling of the zero modes with the relevant operators describing the effect of the lattice artifacts. The actual values of the lattice spacing, the physical volume and the quark mass determine the appropriate power counting, which ought to be used to perform computations using chiral effective theories. 4 Conclusions and outlook

We are establishing the basic knowledge to simulate with Wilson-like fermions in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime. To do this we have introduced a power counting to study the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon expansion with Wilson-like fermions. The LO computation for the chiral condensate confirms the absence of any phase transition, and a NLO extension for the condensate and other observables is currently ongoing.

Numerical simulations in the Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime with Wtm are feasible using a PHMC with exact reweighting. This particular choice allows to lower significantly the quark mass. The extraction of LEC such as Î£Î£\Sigma and Fğ¹F becomes then feasible. Moreover the NLO Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon expansion is not contaminated by chiral logs, which could be a benefit in reducing the systematic errors.

Computations in this extreme region with Wilson-like fermions require a detailed understanding of the usual systematics: discretization errors, quark mass and volume dependence.

We remark that it might be advantageous to combine pğ‘p and Ïµitalic-Ïµ\epsilon regime simulations both as a tool to attack 2+1â€‹(+1)2112+1(+1) simulations, and to further constrain the values of the LEC.
