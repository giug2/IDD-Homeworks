Abstract

Recently, driven by the advances in artificial intelligence, Deep Learning techniques have emerged in financial auditing to examine vast quantities of accounting data. In the last decade, the ongoing digital transformation has fundamentally changed the nature, recording, and volume of audit evidence [82]. Nowadays, organizations record vast quantities of digital accounting records, referred to as Journal Entries (JEs), in Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems [21]. For the audit profession, this unprecedented exposure to large volumes of accounting records offers new opportunities to obtain audit-relevant insights [2]. 2 Related Work

The application of ML in financial audits triggered a sizable body of research by academia [2, 71] and practitioners [72, 16]. This section presents our literature study focusing on the unsupervised, federated, and continual learning of accounting data representations.

Representation Learning denotes a machine learning technique that allows systems to discover relevant data features to solve a given task [6]. Nowadays, most ML methods used in financial audits depend on â€˜humanâ€™ engineered data representations [13]. Such techniques encompass, Naive Bayes classification [5], network analysis [44], univariate and multivariate attribute analysis [3], cluster analysis [73], transaction log mining [33], or business process mining [29, 80]. With the advent of Deep Learning (DL), representation learning techniques have emerged in financial audits [71, 48, 54]. Nowadays, the application of DL in auditing JEs encompasses, autoencoder neural networks [67], adversarial autoencoders [65], or variational autoencoders [86]. Lately, self-supervised learning techniques have been proposed to learn representations for multiple audit tasks [61].

Continual Learning (CL) describes a setting where a model continuously trains on a sequence of experiences [74]. The challenge of forgetting in model learning has been studied extensively [19, 15, 75, 39]. Rehearsal techniques replay samples from past experiences [58, 28, 12] to conduct knowledge distillation [55], while generative rehearsal techniques replay synthesised samples [68]. Recent methods use gradients of previous tasks to mitigate gradient interference [43, 11]. Regularisation techniques consolidate previously acquired knowledge and restrict parameter updates in model optimization. LwF [41], uses knowledge distillation to regularize parameter updates. EWC [35] directly regularises the parameters based on their previous task importance. In SI [84] regularization is applied in separate parameter optimization step. Dynamic architecture techniques prevent forgetting by parameter reuse or increase [59, 47] or. Lately, CL has been deployed in a variety of application scenarios, such as, healthcare [38], machine translation [4], and financial auditing [24].

Federated Learning (FL) enables entities to collaboratively train ML models under the orchestration of a central trusted entity under differential privacy [31, 17]. FedAvg [45] aggregates the clientâ€™s model parameters by computing a weighted average based on the number of training samples. PATE [50] aggregates knowledge transferred from a teacher model that is trained on separated data of student models. FedAdagrad, FedYogi, and FedAdam [56] use adaptive optimizer variations to improve FL convergence. FedProx [40] trains the local with a proximal term that restricts the updates to the central model. FedCurv [69] aims to minimise the model dispersity across clients by adopting a modified version of EWC [35]. Recent works [83, 78] also introduce Bayesian non-parametric aggregation policies. Nowadays, FL learning is applied in sensitive application scenarios, such as, healthcare [14], financial risk modeling [85], and financial auditing [62].

Federated Continual Learning (FCL) addresses the challenge of distributed distribution shifts over time and has only been preliminary studied [9]. Scaffold [32] applies variance reduction in the local updates to correct for a clientâ€™s concept drifts. FedWeIT [81] decompose a model into global shared and sparse task-specific parameters to prevent inter-client interference. CDA-FedAvg [9] extends FedAVG by distribution based drift detection and a long-short term memory for rehearsal. VER [52] conducts an additional server-side rehearsal based training using a VAEâ€™s [34] embedding statistics or actual embeddings. FedDrift [30] uses local drift detection and hierarchical clustering to learn multiple global concept models. To the best of our knowledge, this work presents the first step towards the federated continual learning of highly adaptive audit models in financial auditing.

6 Conclusion

In this work, we proposed financial auditing as a novel application area of Federated Continual Learning (FCL).
